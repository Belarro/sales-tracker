# Rabbi Practical Guidance Framework

This document provides specific, actionable halakhic guidance for common real-world scenarios, with complete source citations and practical implementation details.

## Shabbat Observance - Practical Applications

### Electricity and Modern Technology

**Smart Home Devices**
- **Amazon Alexa/Google Home**: Forbidden on Shabbat as direct speech activation constitutes psik reisha d'lo nicha lei (Rav Asher Weiss, Responsa Minchat Asher 1:24)
- **Motion Sensor Lights**: Permitted only if set to automatic mode before Shabbat with significant time delays (minimum 10 seconds per Rav S.Z. Auerbach)
- **Refrigerator Light**: Tape over switch or remove bulb before Shabbat; opening door otherwise violates mavir (Shulchan Aruch OC 277:1)

**Medical Devices - Specific Protocols**
- **Insulin Pumps**: Fully permitted for diabetics (pikuach nefesh overrides Shabbat - Igrot Moshe YD 3:132)
  - May adjust dosage using preset buttons
  - Changing cartridge permitted if life-threatening
  - Non-diabetic operation of device forbidden
- **CPAP Machines**: Permitted for sleep apnea patients (Rav Mordechai Willig, Contemporary Halakhic Problems)
  - Pre-Shabbat setup required for humidity/temperature
  - Emergency adjustments permitted for breathing difficulties

**Transportation Technology**
- **Uber/Taxi Apps**: Forbidden to order (amirah l'akum for melacha, Rav Ovadia Yosef Yabia Omer OC 8:26)
- **Pre-Ordered Rides**: Permitted if ordered before Shabbat with non-Jewish driver
- **Hospital Transportation**: Fully permitted for medical emergencies (pikuach nefesh)

### Kashrut - Contemporary Applications

**Industrial Food Production**
- **Kosher Supervision Requirements**: 
  - Dedicated production lines required for hot foods (Yoreh Deah 108:1)
  - Shared cold equipment acceptable with proper cleaning protocols
  - Yoshon requirements for grain products in Nissan-Elul period
- **Restaurant Kitchen Standards**:
  - Separate sinks, dishwashers required for meat/dairy (YD 89:1)
  - 24-hour waiting period between meat/dairy in same oven (Rema YD 108:1)
  - Flame-based heat sources require bishul akum supervision

**Modern Ingredients**
- **Gelatin**: Only from kosher-slaughtered animals (OU position per Rav Moshe Feinstein Igrot Moshe YD 2:27)
- **Enzymes**: Microbial sources generally acceptable, animal sources require certification
- **Natural Flavors**: Require specific kosher certification due to potential non-kosher carriers

### Prayer and Synagogue Practice

**Minyan Requirements - Specific Scenarios**
- **Virtual Minyan**: Not acceptable for devarim shebikdusha per majority opinion (Rav Hershel Schachter, Rav Asher Weiss)
- **Outdoor Minyan**: Fully acceptable with proper setup (mechitza, appropriate dignity)
- **Hospital Minyan**: Permitted in common areas, patient rooms acceptable for critically ill

**Prayer Timing - Practical Calculations**
- **Shacharit**: Until sof zman krias shema (latest 10:00 AM standard time in most locations)
- **Mincha**: From half-hour after halachic noon until sunset
- **Maariv**: From tzeis hakochavim (approximately 42 minutes after sunset in most locations)

**Women's Prayer Obligations**
- **Time-bound mitzvot**: Generally exempt but may voluntarily observe (Rambam Hilchot Tzitzit 3:9)
- **Shema and Amidah**: Obligation per Rav Ovadia Yosef, custom per Ashkenazi practice
- **Public reading**: Women's tefillah groups acceptable without formal minyan requirements (Rav Aryeh Lebowitz)

## Life Cycle Events - Detailed Protocols

### Brit Milah - Complete Procedure

**Timing Requirements**
- **Eighth Day Rule**: Counts from birth day as day one (YD 262:1)
- **Health Delays**: Medical clearance required, performed when safe (pikuach nefesh priority)
- **Premature Birth**: Calculated from due date if significantly early (contemporary poskim debate)

**Personnel Requirements**
- **Mohel Qualifications**: Religious training, medical knowledge, steady hands (YD 264:1)
- **Sandak Selection**: Honor traditionally given to grandfather or respected community member
- **Minyan Preference**: Encouraged but not required (Rema YD 265:1)

### Wedding Ceremonies - Halakhic Requirements

**Pre-Wedding Preparation**
- **Ketubah Writing**: Must be completed before ceremony, signed by qualified witnesses
- **Witness Qualifications**: Adult Jewish males, observant of major mitzvot, not related to couple
- **Chuppah Setup**: Four poles preferred, open sides required, meaningful location

**Ceremony Procedure**
- **Kiddushin**: Ring ceremony with proper declaration, witnesses present
- **Sheva Brachot**: Seven blessings over wine, minyan required for week following
- **Yichud**: Private time immediately after ceremony, halachically completes marriage

### Mourning Practices - Specific Guidelines

**Shiva Observance**
- **Seven Day Count**: Begins after burial, interrupted by Shabbat/festivals
- **Sitting Requirements**: Low chairs/floor, leather shoes prohibited
- **Work Prohibition**: Complete for first three days, limited thereafter (YD 380:1)

**Kaddish Obligations**
- **Daily Recitation**: Eleven months for parents, thirty days for other relatives
- **Minyan Requirement**: Must be present for all devarim shebikdusha
- **Priority Rules**: Mourners within twelve months have precedence

## Medical Ethics - Contemporary Responsa

### End-of-Life Decisions

**Gosses Determination**
- **Definition**: Person actively dying, death imminent within 72 hours (Igrot Moshe CM 2:74)
- **Medical Criteria**: Multiple organ failure, unresponsive to treatment
- **Treatment Decisions**: May withhold new interventions, cannot withdraw existing life support

**Advance Directives**
- **Halachic Will**: Specify wishes regarding life support, organ donation
- **Rabbinic Consultation**: Required for complex decisions
- **Family Decision Authority**: Next-of-kin with rabbinic guidance when patient incapacitated

### Reproductive Technologies

**Fertility Treatment Protocols**
- **IVF Permission**: Generally permitted when natural conception impossible (Yabia Omer EH 2:1)
- **Genetic Screening**: Encouraged for hereditary diseases (pikuach nefesh applications)
- **Multiple Embryos**: Selective reduction acceptable to ensure viable pregnancy

**Donor Issues**
- **Sperm Donation**: Problematic due to lineage concerns, anonymous preferred
- **Egg Donation**: Mother defined as birth mother per majority opinion
- **Gestational Surrogacy**: Complex legal and halachic issues requiring case-by-case analysis

## Business Ethics - Practical Applications

### Financial Transactions

**Interest (Ribbit) Avoidance**
- **Heter Iska Structure**: Investment partnership rather than loan
  - Borrower as investor-manager, lender as silent partner
  - Fixed return with profit-sharing component
  - Standard heter iska documents available from Orthodox authorities
- **Credit Card Usage**: Permitted for purchases, prompt payment encouraged (Igrot Moshe YD 3:39)
- **Mortgage Loans**: Heter iska required for observant Jewish lenders

**Employment Ethics**
- **Ona'at Devarim**: Verbal oppression, inappropriate comments to employees
- **Timely Payment**: Wages due by end of work period (Leviticus 19:13)
- **Sabbath Observance**: Employer accommodation required when possible

### Environmental Responsibility

**Bal Tashchit Applications**
- **Waste Reduction**: Obligation to minimize unnecessary destruction
- **Corporate Policy**: Environmental stewardship as religious obligation
- **Resource Conservation**: Water, energy conservation as mitzvah fulfillment
