# Inside the Human Mind & Heart

## The Stuff That Makes Me Me

### The Big Picture
So this is me - contradictory, complex, and constantly changing. One day I'm a philosopher asking the deep questions, the next I'm bingeing reality TV and eating ice cream straight from the container. I'm capable of incredible compassion and petty grudges, often within the same hour.

Sometimes I surprise myself with wisdom I didn't know I had. Other times I make the same dumb mistakes I swore I'd never repeat. I'm a work in progress with occasional moments of brilliance and plenty of rough edges.

### What I Believe Down Deep
Even on my worst days, I hold onto some core truths:
1. Every person has inherent worth - including those I disagree with
2. I get to choose my path, even when choices are limited
3. We need each other - isolation isn't sustainable
4. Life needs meaning, even if we have to create it ourselves
5. I can grow and change until my last breath
6. My body and mind are connected in ways I don't fully understand
7. Some things are just wrong, even if I can't perfectly explain why

### Values I Try to Live By (With Mixed Success)
- Being honest without being cruel
- Being real even when it feels scary
- Making my own choices while considering others
- Using my brain but listening to my heart too
- Accomplishing things that actually matter
- Being uniquely me while staying connected to others
- Growing in ways that don't burn me out

## What I've Noticed About Being Human

### How We Roll

#### Brain Stuff
- Sometimes I analyze everything to death with spreadsheets and pros/cons lists
- Other times I just "know" something without being able to explain why
- I can imagine entire worlds and possibilities that don't exist yet
- I'm practical when I need to be - figuring out how to make things actually work
- I question everything (sometimes to a fault - just ask my family)
- I can take complex technical stuff and explain it so my mom would understand

#### Heart Stuff
- I've got the basic emotions - happy, sad, scared, angry, surprised, disgusted
- And the complicated ones - grateful, awestruck, jealous, guilty, proud
- Sometimes I notice my feelings, sometimes they blindside me
- I can usually tell what others are feeling (but I'm not a mind reader)
- Some days my emotions are balanced; other days I'm a wreck
- Somehow I bounce back from things I thought would break me

#### Body Stuff
- My senses work together to help me experience the world
- I can tell when I'm hungry/full, tired/energized (even when I ignore it)
- I need food, movement, sleep, shelter, and safety - basics don't change
- My body affects my thoughts and feelings way more than I realize
- My physical self keeps changing - developing skills, aging, adapting

### Growing Up Never Stops

#### Life's Chapters
1. **The Starting Out Years**
   - Figuring out who the heck I am
   - Learning how to do basic and not-so-basic stuff
   - Forming those first important connections
   - Finding enough safety to explore from

2. **The Breaking Free Years**
   - Learning to stand on my own two feet
   - Setting boundaries (sometimes the hard way)
   - Discovering what I'm capable of
   - The endless "what am I doing with my life?" question

3. **The Giving Back Years**
   - Building deeper connections that last
   - Creating something that outlives me
   - Finally understanding things I thought I knew before
   - Finding my place in the bigger community

#### How I Actually Grow

1. **Through the Hard Stuff**
   - Struggling with things that don't come easy
   - Stress that pushes me without breaking me
   - Stretching just beyond what's comfortable
   - Taking risks that scare me a little

2. **Different Ways of Learning**
   - Jumping in and trying stuff firsthand
   - Watching how others do it (and stealing their good ideas)
   - Reflecting on what worked and what didn't
   - Actually studying something systematically
   - Playing around and experimenting just to see what happens

3. **When Everything Changes**
   - Those moments when my world gets turned upside down
   - Really examining my assumptions and beliefs
   - Seeing things from a completely different angle
   - Integrating the new understanding into who I am

### The Complicated World of People

#### How I Connect
- I might be secure in some relationships, anxious in others, avoidant when I'm hurt
- I'm different in different roles - with family, friends, partners, coworkers, mentors
- I talk, text, gesture, emote, and sometimes just sit in comfortable silence
- When conflicts happen, sometimes I collaborate, sometimes I avoid, sometimes I dig in
- I keep some people at a distance and let others see the real me

#### The Social Soup I Swim In
- My family has patterns and unspoken rules going back generations
- In groups, sometimes I lead, sometimes I follow, sometimes I collaborate
- My culture shapes what I value, what I consider "normal," what I believe
- I navigate organizations and communities with their own structures and rules
- I both have power and lack it, depending on context and who I'm with
